## ACT 1: THE FALL INTO TWILIGHT (Ch1-13)
*Core Arc: Visual spectacle + class reset + enemies-to-lovers setup*

### Ch1: "The Last Subway"
- **Divergence**: D3 Forbidden Zone + D12 Undercurrent
- **Hook**: Anxiety — Evie's phone dies as the train plunges into darkness
- **Beats**: (1) Evie's PR career collapse montage—betrayed by mentor, dumped by boyfriend, cancelled online; (2) 3AM subway ride, alone, dressed in ruins of a cocktail dress; (3) Platform at Canal St feels *wrong*—too quiet, wrong tile color; (4) A man in a ruined suit (Julian) is already there, staring at a sign that reads "DUSK STATION"; (5) The regular train never comes; (6) The platform extends, lavender light bleeds from nowhere, and a sign flips from "Canal St" to "DUSK STATION — ALL TRAINS INDEFINITELY DELAYED"
- **Sensory**: Subway urine + spilled vodka + ozone → lavender + warm pastry

### Ch2: "The Cat in the Tailcoat"
- **Divergence**: D6 Dual-Line Mirror + D12 Undercurrent
- **Hook**: Curiosity — the Stationmaster cat delivers impossible rules
- **Beats**: (1) Evie and Julian bicker immediately—she's chatty, he's feral; (2) A cat in a tailcoat descends the staircase carrying a silver tray of macarons; (3) The cat speaks: "Welcome to Dusk Station. You are waiting for the Godot. It may come tomorrow. It may never come. The café is self-serving. The wardrobe is infinite. There is no Wi-Fi."; (4) Julian tries to leave—tracks lead to empty fog; (5) Evie eats a macaron and it's the best thing she's ever tasted; (6) The cat places two keys on the bench: "Room 7 and Room 13. Argue among yourselves."
- **Sensory**: Bergamot macaron + hot iron (tracks) + fur + silver polish

### Ch3: "Excel Can Save Your Life"
- **Divergence**: D2 Relationship Fracture + D1 Thread Convergence
- **Hook**: Cognitive — Evie discovers the station's impossible architecture
- **Beats**: (1) Julian has already organized his room, made a schedule, and mapped the platform; (2) Evie's room changes when she's not looking—new dresses appear, the view shifts; (3) She storms into Julian's room demanding answers; (4) He shows her his "Evidence Board" made from station flyers; (5) They explore together: the café refills itself, the bookshop has every book, the record player only plays sad women music; (6) The waiting board flips: "GODOT — STATUS: NOT TODAY. WEATHER: PERPETUAL DUSK."
- **Sensory**: Old paper + vinyl crackle + jasmine + cold marble

### Ch4: "No Signal, No Masters"
- **Divergence**: D7 Dramatic Irony + D3 Forbidden Zone
- **Hook**: Emotional — Evie's first genuine laugh in months
- **Beats**: (1) Evie tries every corner for cell signal—nothing; (2) Julian watches her with disdain, then quietly builds her a paper-clip antenna that obviously won't work; (3) She catches him and they scream-laugh at the absurdity; (4) The cat appears with champagne: "Celebration? Nothing to celebrate. That's the point."; (5) They drink on the platform, feet dangling over tracks; (6) Evie admits her mentor stole her campaign and her boyfriend was in on it. Julian says nothing. Just pours more champagne.
- **Sensory**: Pink champagne bubbles + iron rails + evening air + silk rustle

### Ch5: "The Wardrobe That Reads Your Mind"
- **Divergence**: D12 Undercurrent + D5 Secret Revelation (micro)
- **Hook**: Curiosity — Julian's clothing choices reveal his backstory
- **Beats**: (1) The wardrobe gives Evie a gown that fits like a dream—she twirls; (2) Julian refuses to change from his ruined suit; (3) The wardrobe starts leaving him soft things: cashmere, a thermal, slippers; (4) Evie finds his discarded "clothing gifts" piled outside his door; (5) She confronts him: "Even a magical closet knows you're freezing. Accept the sweater."; (6) He puts on the cashmere and looks so human it hurts her.
- **Sensory**: Cedar + cashmere wool + starched cotton + lavender sachet

### Ch6: "Dancing on Train Tracks"
- **Divergence**: D13 Emotional Payoff (micro) + D6 Dual-Line Mirror

### Ch7: "The Schedule"
- **Divergence**: D2 Relationship Fracture + D10 Ticking Clock (domestic)

- **Beats**: (1) Julian's entire coping mechanism is structure; he makes a schedule for "waiting"; (2) Evie mocks him: "You scheduled *waiting*?"; (3) He defends it aggressively; (4) By afternoon he catches her following his schedule anyway; (5) Their daily routine crystallizes: tea at 7, macarons at 10, walk the tracks at noon, argue at 3, dinner at 7, melancholy at 9; (6) The station clock keeps resetting—time *literally* will not pass.

### Ch12: "The Picnic"
- **Divergence**: D13 Emotional Payoff + D9 Impossible Choice (domestic)

### Ch13: "The Wire"

- **Hook**: Suspense — the board shows their betrayers are looking for them
- **Beats**: (1) The announcement board shows a long crawl: Evie's mentor is under SEC investigation; (2) Julian's ex-boss has been indicted; (3) Their betrayers are suffering *because* they disappeared; (4) The cat: "The Godot comes for those who wait.
